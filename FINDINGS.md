# 5y-backtest research log

This branch is a research artifact. It tests one hypothesis and runs it three different ways. None of the three setups produced lift over a plain rebalanced mean-variance baseline. The findings are preserved here so future work can build on what we learned instead of repeating it.

The forward direction (LLM as watchlist curator rather than expected-return tilt) is being scaffolded on `main`. This branch is frozen.

## The hypothesis

> Business news, properly filtered and digested by an LLM, is a leading indicator of forward equity returns. If a news-researcher LLM can classify each ticker's "wave stage" (buildup / surge / peak / digestion / neutral) from headlines available at time `t`, those classifications should improve portfolio returns when fed into the optimizer as a tilt on expected returns `μ`.

The tilt schedule:

| Stage | Multiplier on μ |
|---|---|
| buildup | 1.20 |
| surge | 1.10 |
| neutral | 1.00 |
| digestion | 0.90 |
| peak | 0.80 |

## The three experiments

All three use mean-variance optimization (λ=1, max_weight=0.25, SPY benchmark), quarterly or monthly rebalancing, and a 1.3y lookback for the price-based μ and Σ. Tilts are applied as a multiplicative bump on μ at each rebalance, using only news classifications dated on or before the rebalance date (strict as-of-date discipline; see `scripts/asof_news_prompt.md`).

### Experiment 1 — 1y backtest with monthly rebalancing

The original headline setup that ships on `main`. Watchlist: the live 11-ticker set. Window: rolling 12 months ending today. Wave classifications: organic from past `/review-portfolio` runs.

**Result: AI tilts subtracted 1.3% over the year vs. the same setup with no tilts.**

### Experiment 2 — 5y backtest, corner-pick starting portfolio, quarterly per-wave classification

Watchlist: 10 tickers (drop ARKG, conditionally drop NUKZ before its 2024-01-24 listing). Window: 2021-09-30 through 2026-04-30 (4.6y). News: 20 quarterly per-wave classifications, each generated by a fresh news-researcher invocation with the seven-lever as-of-date suppression prompt. Aggregated into `data/wave_history_5y.csv`.

**Result: AI tilts subtracted 2.52% over 4.6y.**

The starting portfolio was the optimizer's own day-0 pick, which turned out to be a corner solution that already maxed out AI exposure (NVDA / BOTZ / QTUM / GOOGL each at the 25% cap). That looked like a confound: maybe the optimizer was being graded on a starting point that already absorbed all the AI lift, and the tilts only had downside to add.

### Experiment 3 — 5y backtest, fair-start Sept 2021 portfolio, quarterly per-wave

Same window and wave history as Experiment 2, but starting from a "tech-savvy investor in Sept 2021" portfolio: AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL, SPY, AGG, IAU, BIL with weights typical of that era (i.e., before the post-ChatGPT AI run). The optimizer is given an initial-weights override on day 0 and only starts choosing weights at the second rebalance.

**Result: AI tilts subtracted 4.60% over 4.6y.**

The bias correction made the gap worse, not better.

## Diagnosis

Three things are likely in play:

1. **Wave-level granularity is too coarse.** Every AI-bucket ticker gets the same tilt at each rebalance, even when NVDA's news diverges sharply from GOOGL's. The classification carries less information per ticker than the prompt suggests.

2. **Quarterly cadence is too slow for news.** Most news catalysts move stock prices within days. By the next quarterly rebalance, the price has already absorbed the signal. The optimizer sees the *post*-news μ (since μ is fit from trailing prices) and then applies a *pre*-news tilt on top of it. The tilt and the realized price move are pulling in the same direction, and the tilt becomes double-counting at best, contrarian-signal at worst.

3. **±20% tilt magnitude may not match the real signal magnitude.** Buildup is treated as +20% on μ, but a buildup classification might really be worth +5% (mostly noise) or +50% (when the LLM is genuinely picking up an early adoption inflection). One global multiplier ignores per-ticker information content.

## The per-ticker monthly attempt

To address (1) and (2) we started a follow-up: per-ticker classification (each ticker gets its own stage, decoupled from the wave bucket) at monthly cadence (56 month-end dates 2021-09 through 2026-04). The prompt template is `scripts/asof_news_prompt_per_ticker.md`; aggregator is `scripts/aggregate_wave_history_5y.py` (would need a per-ticker variant). Plan was to also sweep tilt magnitude at ±20% / ±35% / ±50% once the news data was in hand.

**We stopped at 7 of 56 dates** (2021-09-30 through 2022-03-31, captured in `data/news_asof_5y_monthly/`). Rate limits made parallel fan-out unworkable (one early attempt at 56 in parallel returned only 3 successes before the rest hit "you've hit your limit"). The plan shifted to batches of 4, but that's roughly 14 batches sequentially, and we paused to reconsider whether per-ticker monthly tilts were the right experiment to keep funding.

The 7 captured dates are preserved as an example payload set in case anyone wants to resume this thread. The aggregator and backtest path were not updated to consume per-ticker classifications; that work is unfinished.

## What we concluded

Across three setups (1y, 5y corner-pick, 5y fair-start), AI tilts subtracted value consistently. The strength of the optimizer alone is doing the heavy lifting: a hand-curated watchlist plus monthly rebalancing already gives a strong baseline that's hard to beat with classification-based tilts on μ.

That doesn't necessarily mean the news is noise. It means we were asking the LLM to do the wrong job. Classifying wave stages and mapping them to a continuous μ multiplier is a fine-grained numerical task, which is what LLMs are weakest at. A coarser job (yes/no whether a thematic ETF deserves a spot in the watchlist this quarter) better matches LLM strengths.

The next direction on `main`: LLM as watchlist curator. The optimizer runs vanilla mean-variance with no tilts. The LLM monitors news at each rebalance and decides which tickers should be added or removed from a longer candidate universe. Returns lift, if it exists, comes from timing the inclusion/exclusion of thematic ETFs (e.g., rolling NUKZ on around the data-center PPA narrative in late 2024, rolling QTUM on/off based on commercial milestones).
